Section 3

Firebase Usage

This App uses Google Firebase, a mobile development platform provided by Google LLC. Firebase provides two specific services in this App:

Firebase Analytics

Firebase Analytics helps us understand how users interact with the App in aggregate. For example, it tells us which practice topics are most popular, how long a typical study session lasts, and which features are used most. This helps us prioritise improvements.

The data collected by Firebase Analytics is anonymised and aggregated. We cannot identify you as an individual from this data. Google may use this data in accordance with its own privacy policy.

Anonymised Aggregated only No personal profiles

Firebase Crashlytics

Firebase Crashlytics automatically captures crash reports when the App encounters a technical error. These reports include information about the type of device, the Android version, and the sequence of events leading up to the crash. This allows us to identify and fix bugs quickly so you have a stable experience.

Crash reports do not include your personal information. They are technical diagnostics only.

Bug reports Device diagnostics No personal data

ℹ️ For full details on how Google processes data through Firebase, please review Google's Firebase Privacy and Security documentation and Google's Privacy Policy.

Section 4

AdMob & Advertising

This App is free to use. To support ongoing development, we display advertisements provided by Google AdMob, an advertising service operated by Google LLC.

How Advertising Works

AdMob may display banner advertisements and interstitial (full-screen) advertisements within the App. Google's advertising technology may use your device's resettable Advertising Identifier (Google Advertising ID) and other non-personal signals to show ads that are relevant to your interests.

Personalised vs Non-Personalised Ads

Where required by law — including for users in the United Kingdom and European Economic Area (EEA) — the App presents a consent prompt on first launch powered by Google's User Messaging Platform (UMP). Your choice determines whether personalised or non-personalised advertisements are shown to you:

  • Personalised ads: Ads selected using your Advertising ID and interest signals. You can opt out at any time by resetting your Google Advertising ID in your device Settings.
  • Non-personalised ads: Ads based only on the context of the App (an educational app about UK citizenship), without using your Advertising ID for targeting.

Regardless of your choice, the App continues to function fully and no features are locked behind ad consent.

Your Advertising Choices

  • Reset your Google Advertising ID: Settings → Google → Ads → Reset advertising ID
  • Opt out of personalised ads: Settings → Google → Ads → Opt out of Ads Personalisation
  • Visit the Google Ad Settings page to manage your preferences
  • Visit Your Online Choices for additional opt-out options (EEA users)
⚠️ We do not control the content of advertisements served by AdMob. Ad content is determined by Google and its advertising partners. For details on Google's advertising practices, please visit How Google uses information from sites or apps that use our services.

Section 5

Offline Data Storage

A significant portion of the App's functionality works entirely offline. Your study progress, quiz results, bookmarks, and settings are stored locally on your device using an on-device database (Android Room) and Android's DataStore. This data never leaves your device to our servers.

What Is Stored Locally

  • Your practice session history and accuracy statistics
  • Mock test scores and pass/fail records
  • Questions you have bookmarked for revision
  • Your daily study streak and XP (experience points)
  • App settings (theme, font size, sound preference)
  • Recently viewed questions for smart revision
  • Achievement progress and unlocked badges

Cloud Backup

Android's Auto Backup feature may back up some app data to your Google account as part of the standard Android backup system. This is controlled by your device's backup settings and Google account preferences, not by us. The backed-up data contains only your study progress — no personal information beyond what you have stored within the App's features.

Sensitive progress data (your test score history stored in the local database) is excluded from cloud backup by design to protect your privacy.

✅ Your progress data stays on your device. We do not have a server-side account system and we cannot access your quiz scores, bookmarks, or study history.

Section 8

Children's Privacy

The Life in the UK citizenship test is an official requirement for adults aged 18 and over who are applying for British citizenship or indefinite leave to remain. Accordingly, this App is designed for and directed at adults.

The App is not specifically directed at children under the age of 13 (or under 16 in jurisdictions that apply a higher threshold under GDPR). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

If you are a parent or guardian and you believe your child has used this App and provided personal information, please contact us immediately at the address in Section 12 and we will take prompt steps to address this.

The advertising configuration in the App has been set to indicate that the App is not child-directed and that targeted advertising to users under the relevant age threshold is not appropriate. We do not enrol this App in Google Play's Designed for Families programme.

Section 9

Security

We take reasonable and appropriate technical measures to protect the information processed by this App:

  • All network communication uses HTTPS/TLS encryption. The App does not permit unencrypted HTTP connections.
  • Local data is stored in Android's protected app-private storage, which is not accessible to other apps on your device.
  • The App requests only the minimum permissions necessary to function — specifically, internet access for analytics and ads. No sensitive permissions (camera, microphone, contacts, precise location) are requested.
  • The released App binary is compiled with code shrinking and obfuscation, reducing the risk of reverse-engineering.
⚠️ While we implement strong security practices, no method of electronic transmission or storage is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security, but we are committed to protecting your information to the best of our ability and to notifying you of any significant security incident where required by law.
